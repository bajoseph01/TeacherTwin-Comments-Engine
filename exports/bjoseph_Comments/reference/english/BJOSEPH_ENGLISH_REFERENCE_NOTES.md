## How To Use These JSONs

1. Use the English marksheet as the primary evidence for performance, strengths, and development areas.
2. Use the student-voice JSON only to add one or two safe personal sentences or details that make the comment feel known and specific.
3. Do not force a personal detail into every comment if the match is weak or the evidence confidence is low.
4. If a detail feels too private, too exact, or too family-specific, generalise it before using it.
5. When the voice reference and the marksheet pull in different directions, trust the marksheet for academic reporting and use the voice reference only for warmth or individuality.

## Safety Guardrails

1. Do not repeat exact street numbers, exact home addresses, or overly specific family details.
2. Do not infer personality or behaviour beyond what is written.
3. For low- or medium-confidence records, stay with safer hobbies, interests, or broad personal themes.
4. Turn writing needs into gentle teacher language such as "is working on", "is continuing to develop", or "will benefit from".
